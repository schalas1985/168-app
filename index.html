What values are
Not goals. Not rules. Direction.

Values are not things you achieve. They are the way you want to move through life. "Health" isn't a value. "Moving my body with intention and care" is a value. A goal can be completed and forgotten. A value is always alive.

They drive your commitments, your map, and your reflections.

You do not need to have this figured out. Come here when you are ready, or when something in your life is asking you to look. You are welcome to begin exploring your values with curiosity by diving into the Values Canvas below.

Values Canvas
1
Where are you now?
Rate each area 1 to 10 for importance and how much you are living it. The shortfall shows where values work matters most.

Adapted from the Valued Living Questionnaire (ACT). Stays on your device.

2
What stirs something?
Tap any words that resonate. You can pick as many as you like. For each one you tap, write what it means to you specifically.

From Marsha Linehan's values framework (DBT). These are prompts, not prescriptions.

3
Three questions
Take your time. Write freely. There are no right answers here.
What do you keep coming back to, even when it costs you something?
When you feel most like yourself, what are you doing, and who are you being with?
If you had one year left, how would you spend your time, and what does that say about what matters most?

If these questions feel impossible right now, that is okay. It may mean there is something still in the way. Consider speaking with someone you trust before returning. There is no wrong time to come back.

4
Write your first value
From the I. In your own words. As honest as you can be right now.

"I choose to show up fully for the people I love."

"My body deserves consistent care and movement."

"I create things that are worth making."

Your values will change over time. That is not inconsistency, it is growth. Every version you write is kept so you can see how you have moved.
